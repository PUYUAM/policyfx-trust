### 📸 Post 2 — “Why ‘Ah’ Names Don’t Work for Adults”

> 💡 Real talk: “Ah Li”, “Ah Mei” — they’re affectionate. But not *respectful*.

If your Chinese partner’s family calls you “Ah Li”, it’s like calling a doctor “Doc” at their own wedding.

A proper two-character name changes everything:
• Jiǎn Héng (简衡) = “Simple balance” → signals calm competence
• Yàn Qīng (砚清) = “Inkstone clarity” → honors depth & craft

It’s not about sounding Chinese. It’s about being *seen* — clearly, seriously, kindly.
